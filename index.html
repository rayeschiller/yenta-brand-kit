02   Text Types — How They're Different
01
Clip Hook Text
wait — she said WHAT 👀
Where: First 1–3 sec of a Reel or TikTok
Font: Chalkboard SE · Bold
Size: Large — fills the frame
Color: One brand color (pink, orange, or blue)
Background: White
Length: 1–2 lines max, punchy
02
Clip Caption Text
"and honestly the audacity of it all —"
Where: On-screen text overlay during video
Font: Chalkboard SE · Regular
Size: Medium — readable but not dominant
Color: Black body + one brand color for emphasis words
Background: Transparent over video
Length: Follows speech rhythm, phrase by phrase
03
Post Caption Text
new ep is out now ✡️
we went fully unhinged this week and i'm not sorry.

🔗 link in bio
#yentapod #gossip
Where: Written caption below IG post or TikTok
Font: Chalkboard SE (typed, not overlaid)
Size: Standard caption — platform default
Color: Black body + brand colors for highlight words
Background: N/A — platform caption area
Length: 3–6 lines + hashtags

04   Brand Usage Guidelines
✅ Do
  • Use Chalkboard font for all post text
  • Use white for all hook card backgrounds
  • Use only the three brand colors for highlighted or hook text
  • Keep caption tone casual, witty, and authentic
  • Rotate highlight colors across posts for variety
  • Use one color per post to stay clean
  • Add emojis to reinforce the brand's queer + Jewish warmth
🚫 Don't
  • Don't use colored backgrounds on hook cards
  • Don't mix multiple highlight colors in a single sentence
  • Don't use other fonts — Chalkboard only
  • Don't write captions in a formal or corporate voice
  • Don't over-explain — leave room for curiosity
  • Don't use low-contrast text (e.g. orange on white for body copy)
✍️ Voice & Tone
  • Write like you're texting your funniest friend
  • Lowercase is welcome — adds personality
  • Short sentences hit harder than long ones
  • Lean into the gossip + community angle
  • Humor first, information second
